通用电气 (General Electric) 的衰落：数字化转型失败的商业评论 The Fall of General Electric: A Business Review in Digital Transformation Failure

亚利桑那州立大学 | 2025年11月26日 Arizona State University | November 26, 2025

本文分析了通用电气 (GE) 在首席执行官杰夫·伊梅尔特领导下数字化转型失败的原因。文章探讨了通用电气雄心勃勃的愿景——从一家传统制造商转型为“十大软件公司”——以及在市场推广执行方面的战略失误，最终导致这家美国最具标志性的企业集团之一分崩离析的文化脱节。 This essay analyzes the failed digital transformation of General Electric (GE) under CEO Jeff Immelt. It explores the ambitious vision to modernize from a traditional manufacturer to a "top ten software company," the strategic missteps in go-to-market execution, and the cultural disconnect that by the end unraveled one of America's most iconic conglomerates.

利用高斯定律分析 p-n 结中的电场和电势 Analysis of the Electric Field and Potential in a p-n Junction Using Gauss's Law

亚利桑那州立大学 | 2025年7月30日 Arizona State University | July 30, 2025

本分析结合高斯定律和耗尽近似来模拟半导体 p-n 结的内部静电场。该基础模型通过假设结周围区域完全没有移动电子和空穴，从而简化了系统，只留下固定电离原子的简单电荷分布。将高斯定律应用于该电荷分布可以推导出结的电场，该电场呈现三角形分布，并在物理界面处达到峰值。对该电场进行积分可以得出电势，该电势在耗尽区呈现二次分布。分析最终得出内建电势 (V_bi) 的表达式，这是一个关键参数，它将结的固有电压与半导体的掺杂特性直接联系起来，对于解释二极管的整流作用至关重要。 This analysis uses Gauss's Law in conjunction with the depletion approximation to model the internal electrostatics of a semiconductor p-n junction. This foundational model simplifies the system by assuming the region around the junction is completely devoid of mobile electrons and holes, leaving a straightforward charge distribution of fixed ionized atoms. Applying Gauss's Law to this charge distribution allows for the derivation of the junction's electric field, which is shown to have a triangular profile that peaks at the physical interface. Integrating this electric field then yields the electric potential, which has a quadratic profile across the depletion region. The analysis culminates in an expression for the built-in potential (V_bi), a key parameter that connects the junction's inherent voltage directly to the semiconductor's doping properties and is crucial for explaining the rectifying action of diodes.

CIS 310 - 商业数据可视化：可视化的六项原则 (理论实践) CIS 310 - Business Data Visualization: The Six Lessons of Visualization (Theory in Action)

2025年8月 - 2025年12月 Aug 2025 - Dec 2025

本项目全面探索数据可视化和视觉叙事，利用 Microsoft Excel 和 Tableau 将原始数据集转化为可行的商业洞察。项目从基础技术入手，包括数据准备以及创建条形图、折线图和散点图等标准图表，并逐步深入到更复杂的分析图表，例如斜率图、瀑布图和帕累托分析。项目的核心重点是应用数据叙事的“六项原则”，需要策略性地运用设计原则来简化图表、建立视觉层次结构并为特定受众定义上下文。项目最终将开发出连贯的叙事和交互式仪表盘，将分析结果整合为具有说服力的战略建议，例如根据绩效趋势确定需要“放弃”、“奖励”或“干预”的区域。 This project represents a comprehensive exploration of data visualization and visual storytelling, utilizing both Microsoft Excel and Tableau to transform raw datasets into actionable business insights. Starting with foundational techniques, including data preparation and the creation of standard visuals like bar, line, and scatter plots, the work advances to complex analytical displays such as slopegraphs, waterfall charts, and Pareto analyses. A core focus is placed on applying the "Six Lessons" of data storytelling, requiring the strategic use of design principles to declutter visuals, establish visual hierarchy, and define context for specific audiences. The project concludes in the development of cohesive narratives and interactive dashboards that synthesize findings into persuasive strategic recommendations, such as identifying regions to "Abandon," "Reward," or "Intervene" based on performance trends.

高级科学计算：基于 MATLAB 的数值分析与模拟 Advanced Scientific Computing: Numerical Analysis and Simulation with MATLAB

2025年9月 Sep 2025

本项目全面探索了基于 MATLAB 的科学计算和数值分析，弥合了基础算法理论与复杂物理系统模拟之间的鸿沟。该项目系统地解决了三个核心问题：非线性动力学和混沌的可视化，通过逻辑斯蒂映射的分岔、牛顿分形和洛伦兹系统的奇异吸引子进行阐释；关键数值方法的应用，包括谱分析、样条插值和求根算法；以及常微分方程和偏微分方程的精确求解。通过实施诸如 Crank-Nicolson 离散化、交替方向隐式 (ADI) 离散化和流函数-涡度公式等先进的离散化技术，本项目成功地模拟了多种随时间变化的现象，涵盖了从天体力学和热扩散到不可压缩流体流动中特有的非定常涡脱落等诸多领域。 This project presents a comprehensive exploration of scientific computing and numerical analysis using MATLAB. It bridges the gap between fundamental algorithmic theory and the simulation of complex physical systems. The work systematically addresses three core things: the visualization of nonlinear dynamics and chaos, illustrated through the bifurcation of the logistic map, Newton fractals, and the strange attractors of the Lorenz system; the application of essential numerical methods, including spectral analysis, spline interpolation, and root-finding algorithms; and the accurate solution of ordinary and partial differential equations. By implementing advanced discretization techniques such as the Crank-Nicolson, Alternating Direction Implicit (ADI), and stream function-vorticity formulations, the project successfully models diverse time-dependent phenomena, ranging from celestial mechanics and heat diffusion to the unsteady vortex shedding characteristic of incompressible fluid flow.
